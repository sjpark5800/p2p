	<Panel left="518" right="937" width="447" height="166">
		<Text>Conclusions</Text>
		<Text>Emissions from AgInSe</Text>
		<Text>2 are more uniform both spatially and spectrally than Cu-containing samples</Text>
		<Text>AgInSe</Text>
		<Text>2 less affected by reduced emission from surface features – partly due to less surface faceting</Text>
		<Text>Cu-containing samples exhibit enhanced luminescence from grain boundary or inter-grain areas</Text>
		<Text>•Both Cu-containing samples exhibit localized luminescent variations indicative of compositional</Text>
		<Text>fluctuations or electrically active defect fluctuations</Text>
		<Text>•As Cu increases, emission gets broader indicating larger number of specific defect states (more local</Text>
		<Text>chemical variation)</Text>
		<Text>•Device implications: AIS may produce more uniform cell performance and may exhibit less air-</Text>
		<Text>sensitivity during manufacture</Text>
	</Panel>
